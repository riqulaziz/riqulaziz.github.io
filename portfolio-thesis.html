Diabetes Detection Non-Invasive Approach

Using Extreme Learning Machine (ELM)

Diabetes mellitus is one of the 4 leading causes of death in the world due to non-communicable diseases. In Indonesia, cases of diabetes ranks 10 major cases of the disease that affects many people, from teenagers to the elderly.

The process of measuring the level of diabetes mellitus, which is still invasive, also causes people to rarely carry out regular tests, besides because it also requires knowledge to translate the meaning of the measurement results.

Based on these problems, this thesis project seeks to take a more non-invasive and fast approach using machine learning. I later adopted this project as a thesis project entitled "Implementation of Extreme Learning Machine on Photoplethysmograph Signals for Non-Invasive Diabetes Mellitus Detection".

By using secondary data, the system accuracy results are 94.5% with a system sensitivity and specificity of more than 90%.
